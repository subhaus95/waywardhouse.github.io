## Why this matters

Geography asks an important question:

Why do patterns occur where they do?

Spatial models attempt to explain patterns such as:

- city growth
- river networks
- vegetation zones
- transportation systems

---

## Spatial variables

In spatial models we often represent location using coordinates:

$$
(x, y)
$$

Each point represents a position in space.

---

## Distance

Distance between two points is given by

$$
d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2}
$$

This formula appears throughout geographic modelling.

---

## Geography example

Transportation planning often uses distance models to determine optimal locations for infrastructure such as hospitals, warehouses, and emergency response centres.
