## Why this matters

Some processes do not grow steadily.

Instead they grow **faster and faster**.

Population growth, compound interest, and disease spread are classic examples.

These processes follow exponential laws.

---

## Exponential growth

A basic exponential function looks like

$$
y = a e^{kt}
$$

where

- $a$ = starting value
- $k$ = growth rate
- $t$ = time

---

## Example

If bacteria double every hour,

$$
N = N_0 2^t
$$

After three hours:

$$
N = N_0 8
$$

The population has multiplied eightfold.

---

## Geography example

Algal blooms in lakes can grow exponentially when nutrients suddenly increase.

Monitoring programs use exponential models to estimate how quickly blooms will expand.
