## Why this matters

Scientists often want to know whether two variables move together. Correlation measures the strength of this relationship.

## Correlation coefficient

$$
r = \frac{cov(X,Y)}{\sigma_X \sigma_Y}
$$

Values lie between -1 and 1.

## Interpretation

| r | Meaning |
|---|---------|
|1|perfect positive relationship|
|0|no relationship|
|-1|perfect negative relationship|

## Geography example

Satellite vegetation indices often correlate strongly with rainfall patterns in semi‑arid regions.
