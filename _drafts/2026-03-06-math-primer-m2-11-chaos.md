## Why this matters

Some systems behave in ways that appear random even though the underlying equations are deterministic.

This behaviour is known as **chaos**.

Chaotic systems are extremely sensitive to initial conditions.

---

## Sensitivity to initial conditions

Small differences at the beginning of a system can grow rapidly.

This idea is sometimes called the **butterfly effect**.

Even tiny measurement errors can lead to large prediction differences.

---

## Example

Weather systems are famously chaotic.

Small uncertainties in atmospheric conditions can produce very different forecasts over time.

---

## Geography example

River channel evolution can display chaotic behaviour due to nonlinear interactions between flow, sediment transport, and bank erosion.
