## Why this matters

Many real-world decisions involve choosing the best possible outcome.

Examples include:

- minimizing transportation costs
- maximizing crop yield
- optimizing reservoir management
- selecting locations for renewable energy infrastructure

Mathematics provides tools to analyze these decisions.

---

## The basic idea

Optimization asks:

What value of a variable produces the best outcome?

For example:

$$
f(x)
$$

might represent cost.

The goal could be to find the value of x that minimizes cost.

---

## Example

Suppose transportation cost depends on distance travelled.

If the cost function is

$$
C = 5d + 100
$$

minimizing distance minimizes cost.

---

## Geography example

Wind farm placement often involves optimization models that balance wind availability, land cost, and grid connection distance.
