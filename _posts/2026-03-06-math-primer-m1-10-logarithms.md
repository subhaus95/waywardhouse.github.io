## Why this matters

When numbers grow extremely large or small, logarithms help keep them manageable.

Earthquake magnitude, sound intensity, and pH all use logarithmic scales.

---

## Definition

A logarithm answers the question:

> What exponent produces a number?

Example

$$
10^2 = 100
$$

So

$$
\log_{10} 100 = 2
$$

---

## Example: pH

The acidity of water is measured using

$$
pH = -\log_{10}[H^+]
$$

A drop of one pH unit means acidity increases tenfold.

---

## Geography example

Earthquake magnitude uses a logarithmic scale.

An increase of one unit represents roughly **ten times** greater ground motion.
