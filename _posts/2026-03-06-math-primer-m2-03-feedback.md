## Why this matters

Many systems influence their own behaviour.

When outputs feed back into inputs we call this **feedback**.

Feedback can stabilize systems or push them toward instability.

---

## Positive feedback

Positive feedback amplifies change.

Example:

- melting ice reduces reflectivity
- darker surface absorbs more heat
- additional melting occurs

---

## Negative feedback

Negative feedback stabilizes systems.

Example:

A thermostat maintains room temperature by turning heating on and off.

---

## Mathematical representation

Dynamic systems often use recursive equations:

$$
x_{t+1} = f(x_t)
$$

The next state depends on the previous state.

---

## Geographic case study

In arid landscapes vegetation loss can trigger positive feedback between soil erosion and reduced rainfall infiltration, accelerating desertification.
