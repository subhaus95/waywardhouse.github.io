## About This Series

The **Computational Geography Laboratory** is a structured curriculum that teaches geography through mathematical modeling and computational thinking. Unlike traditional textbooks, this is a modular system where each model is self-contained but part of a coherent learning architecture.

**Core Philosophy:**
- Geography is a modeling discipline, not just memorization
- Mathematics should be derived, not asserted
- Computation makes models tangible
- Physical and human geography share mathematical structures
- Visualization deepens understanding

---

## How to Use This Series

### Three Ways to Read

1. **Linear progression:** Read Models 1–12 in order for a complete introduction
2. **Thematic clusters:** Focus on one cluster (A–E) for depth in that topic
3. **Curated pathways:** Follow recommended sequences based on your goals

### Skill Requirements

Each model includes:
- **Difficulty rating** (1–5): Indicates mathematical sophistication required
- **Math prerequisites:** What you need to know before starting
- **Tagged skills:** Mathematical concepts, spatial reasoning, computational techniques

### CLUSTER A — Modeling Change

**Focus:** How things grow, decay, and reach equilibrium over time

<div class="essay-list">

**Model 1: [What Is a Spatial Model?](/essays/01-what-is-spatial-model/)**  
*Variables, units, proportional reasoning, scalar fields*  
Difficulty: ★☆☆☆☆ | No prerequisites

Difficulty: ★★☆☆☆ | Requires: Basic algebra

**Model 4: [Logistic Growth and Equilibrium](/essays/04-logistic-growth-and-equilibrium/)**  
*Nonlinear functions, carrying capacity, stability*  
Difficulty: ★★★☆☆ | Requires: Model 3

## Curated Learning Pathways

### Pathway 1: Rebuilding Algebra Through Geography

*For learners rebuilding mathematical confidence*

1. Model 1: What Is a Model?
2. Model 2: Linear Change and Rate
3. Model 3: Exponential Growth
4. Model 10: Gravity Models

**Why this sequence:** Builds from simple proportions to exponentials, with minimal calculus. Geography provides motivation and context.

---

### Pathway 2: Introduction to Differential Equations

*For learners ready for calculus concepts*

1. Model 3: Exponential Growth
2. Model 4: Logistic Growth
3. Model 5: Light Attenuation

**Why this sequence:** Three archetypal differential equations: exponential, logistic, spatial exponential decay.

*For technically-minded learners wanting to understand spatial analysis*

2. Model 8: Gradient and Aspect

**Why this sequence:** Mathematical foundations of GIS terrain analysis. From continuous surfaces to discrete algorithms.

## Design Principles

1. **Mathematics must be derived, not asserted**  
   Every equation is built from first principles or clearly stated assumptions.

2. **Units must always be tracked**  
   Dimensional analysis prevents errors and clarifies meaning.

3. **Vector geometry introduced visually before symbolically**  
   Geometric intuition precedes algebraic manipulation.

4. **Human geography treated quantitatively**  
   Social and economic processes follow mathematical patterns too.

5. **Interaction used only when it deepens understanding**  
   Visualizations serve pedagogy, not decoration.

6. **Models remain modular; coherence emerges through tagging**  
   You can start anywhere, but structured pathways guide progression.
