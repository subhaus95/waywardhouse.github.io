## Why this matters

Most models in science are ultimately built around one idea: **a function**.

A function is simply a rule that connects one quantity to another.

Temperature depends on altitude.  
Population depends on time.  
River discharge depends on rainfall and watershed area.

Instead of describing these relationships with words, mathematics expresses them with functions.

Once we have a function, we can predict behaviour, explore scenarios, and build models of real systems.

---

## The main idea

A function describes how one variable determines another.

We often write this relationship as

$$
y = f(x)
$$

This means: *the value of y depends on x according to some rule f.*

---

## Example

Consider the function

$$
y = 2x
$$

If

$$
x = 3
$$

then

$$
y = 6
$$

If

$$
x = 5
$$

then

$$
y = 10
$$

Each input produces exactly one output.

That property defines a function.

---

## Worked example: water storage

Suppose a reservoir stores water according to

$$
V = 12h
$$

where

- $V$ = water volume
- $h$ = water height

If water level is 4 m:

$$
V = 48
$$

If water level is 6 m:

$$
V = 72
$$

The function connects water height to stored volume.

---

## Geography example

In mountainous terrain temperature often decreases with altitude.

A simplified model is

$$
T = 15 - 0.0065h
$$

where

- $T$ = temperature
- $h$ = altitude

This is a function linking altitude to atmospheric temperature.
