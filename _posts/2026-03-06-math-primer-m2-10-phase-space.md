## Why this matters

Instead of plotting a variable against time, we can sometimes gain deeper insight by plotting variables **against each other**.

This type of diagram is called **phase space**.

It shows the trajectory of a system as it evolves.

---

## Phase space

Suppose a system has two variables:

- x
- y

Instead of graphing each variable separately through time, we plot

$$
(x, y)
$$

as a point in a coordinate plane.

As the system evolves, the point moves through space, tracing a trajectory.

---

## Example

Predator–prey systems are often represented in phase space.

The interaction between predator populations and prey populations produces characteristic cycles.

---

## Geography example

Climate models sometimes examine temperature and ocean circulation strength together in phase space to explore stability.
